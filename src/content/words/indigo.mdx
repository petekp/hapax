## noun

A deep blue dye and the color associated with it. Dark, cool, and saturated, hovering between blue and violet like twilight made pigment (art; also general).

> *The indigo cloth held the day’s last light, a blue so dense it felt almost like depth rather than color.*

## adjective

Of that deep blue-violet hue.

> *She wore an indigo coat that made the winter sky look suddenly pale.*

## Etymology

From Latin **indicum** “Indian (dye),” via Greek and later European trade terms, naming a pigment long imported from South Asia. The color’s name is a map of commerce: hue carried across oceans in the holds of ships.

## Related Words

- **aniline** (a later industrial dye world, historically associated with blues)
- **ultramarine** (another storied blue, literally “beyond the sea”)
- **woad** (a European blue dye; older local counterpart)
- **azure** (a lighter blue; a different altitude of color)
