## verb

1. Twist or pull forcibly, often against resistance; to wrench something into a new position (formal).

> *He wrested the jammed window upward, the frame protesting with a long reluctant groan.*

2. Seize, extort, or take by force; to obtain what is not freely given (formal).

> *She wrested an apology from him, and it sounded more like surrender than remorse.*

## Etymology

From Old English **wræstan** “to twist, torture,” related to *wrist* and *writhe*. The word keeps the body’s torque inside it.

## Related Words

- **wrench** (close mechanical near-synonym)
- **extort** (take by pressure; more legal-moral)
- **writhe** (twist in motion; kin by root)
- **wrestle** (same family, more sportive)
