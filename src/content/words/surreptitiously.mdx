## adverb

In a stealthy, unnoticed manner, with concealment as the chosen route rather than an afterthought. **Surreptitiously** implies strategy, movement designed to slip past attention.

> *She surreptitiously slid the key back onto the hook, as if the house itself might accuse her.*

## Etymology

From **surreptitious** + **-ly**, carrying forward the Latin sense of “snatched away” into the grammar of action.

## Related Words

- **furtively** (by quick, sidelong manner)
- **secretly** (neutral umbrella term)
- **stealthily** (emphasizes skill at avoiding detection)
- **underhandedly** (suggests moral taint)
