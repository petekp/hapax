## noun

1. A small bundle or cluster—especially a bundle of anatomical fibers (as in a nerve fascicle) or a tight grouping of flowers or leaves (technical).

> *Under magnification the nerve resolved into fascicles, each a cord of smaller threads bound into purpose.*

2. A separately issued installment of a book or scholarly work; a part published as a discrete section (academic).

> *The dictionary arrived in fascicles, each slim booklet a promise of the larger edifice to come.*

## Etymology

From Latin **fasciculus** “little bundle,” diminutive of **fascis** “bundle.” The word has carried “bundling” from rods and reeds into nerves, petals, and pages.

## Related Words

- **fascicle** (in anatomy, often contrasted with larger **tracts**)
- **fascicle** (in publishing, akin to an **installment**)
- **fascicle**’s root **fascis** (the larger “bundle” behind it)
- **fasciculate** (arranged in bundles; botanical and anatomical use)
