## noun

Nearness in space, time, or relationship; closeness that exerts its own quiet influence. **Propinquity** often hints at consequence, how being near rearranges attention, affection, or fate.

> *Their propinquity turned acquaintance into intimacy, the way shared hallways turn strangers into stories.*

## Etymology

From Latin **propinquitas** "nearness," from **propinquus** "near." Always about closeness that counts, distance measured not in meters but in how easily lives touch.

## Related Words

- **proximity** (neutral, spatial near-synonym)
- **contiguity** (touching; stricter physical adjacency)
- **kinship** (relational closeness; one domain of propinquity)
- **vicinity** (neighborhood nearness; more concrete)
